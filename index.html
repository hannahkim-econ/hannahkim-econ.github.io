Research

Replanting Under Risk: Weather Shocks and Portfolio Choice in Perennial Agriculture

Job market paper  ·  Solo authored

Job market paper Ethiopia · LSMS-ISA
Draft (available upon request)
This paper studies how smallholder farmers adjust long-term crop choices in response to weather shocks. Using data from Ethiopian coffee farmers, I examine how weather-induced changes in expected profits and uncertainty affect decisions to replant or switch to alternative crops. The findings provide evidence on climate adaptation behavior and highlight trade-offs between farmers' responses and broader policy goals.

Global Extreme Weather and Early Childhood Undernourishment

with Andrew Hultgren and Sarah Janzen

Working paper DHS · ERA5 · 56+ countries
Draft (available upon request)
This paper estimates the causal effects of prenatal and early-life extreme weather exposure on child nutritional status across 56+ low- and middle-income countries, linking ERA5 weather data to DHS child-level data. Extreme heat exacerbates the risk of stunting, underweight, and wasting, with effects strongest among toddlers aged 12–36 months; drought increases underweight risk and heavy rainfall raises wasting, particularly in rural households.

Wildfire Damages in the United States

with Andrew Hultgren

Working paper MODIS · county-level panel
This paper estimates the economic impacts of wildfire exposure using county-level panel data in the United States. It constructs a LitPop-weighted measure of wildfire exposure from MODIS burned area data and applies distributed lag models to capture wildfire impacts. The results show limited population responses, declines in unemployment consistent with post-fire reconstruction, and heterogeneous effects across high- and low-fire-frequency regions.
